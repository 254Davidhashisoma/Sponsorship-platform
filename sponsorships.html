Access Programs

Moringa School is a coding school that provides technology and professional training to students, through intensive rapid skills training programs. Moringa is developing the next generation of world-class software developers through market-driven education. The school currently offers a ready-to-work model, training students in vocational IT skills (Moringa offers classes in software development and data science) and soft skills (e.g., leadership and teamwork) to prepare them to work effectively. Mastercard Scholarship

The Mastercard Foundation Scholars Program is developing Africa’s next generation of leaders. The program allows students whose talent and promise exceed their financial resources to complete their education. With a vision that education is a catalyst for social and economic change, the program focuses on developing leaders who are transformative, encouraging them to be active contributors in their communities. Google Africa Developer Scholarship

Registration is now open for the Google Africa Developer Scholarship program supported by Grow with Google, Andela, and Pluralsight. Software engineers (and aspiring software engineers) looking to build competencies in Android, Google Cloud, and Mobile Web (MWS) are encouraged to apply to the program. The aim of this program is to continuously engage with aspiring and existing software engineers on these tracks a nd equip them with the requisite skills to get certified. Daraja Academy

Daraja Academy in Kenya provides an exemplary secondary education to girls who have no other means to attend school. They are girls who—despite their circumstances—earn high grades in primary school, give back to their communities, and demonstrate leadership. They show courage and kindness when faced with adversity. They are the kind of girls who can disrupt the cycle of poverty and act as champions of equality if only given the chance. One day, we hope that every girl will receive the education she needs to better her life, her community, and her world. Read more About the Program

THE FLEISCHER FOUNDATION

The Fleischer Foundation works to empower children from the Mukuru Kwa Njenga slum of Nairobi, Kenya through education and mentorship. We are a not-for-profit (NFP) organization that is incorporated in Kenya and in the United States as a 501(c)(3) tax-exempt charity. Our Founder, Stephen Fleischer, was born and raised in New York City. After his undergraduate studies at Loyola University Maryland, he took a year to volunteer as a teacher at a Marianist elementary school in a slum of Nairobi. Read more About the Program
